Advancing Pulmonary Research

At the Ji Laboratory, we study lung pathophysiology, focusing on ARDS, COVID-19, and sarcoidosis through cutting-edge multi-omics approaches and humanized mouse models.

Explore Our Research

About Our Lab

The Ji Laboratory in the Department of Surgery at Loyola University Chicago's Stritch School of Medicine is led by Dr. James (Hong-Long) Ji, MD, PhD. Our research focuses on understanding the molecular mechanisms behind pulmonary conditions such as ARDS, COVID-19, and sarcoidosis.

Through innovative techniques including scRNAseq, multi-omics analysis, metaproteomics, machine learning, and 3D organoids, we aim to identify new endotypes, regenerative pathways, biomarkers, and host-pathogen interactions that can lead to novel therapeutic approaches.

Our lab has established humanized mouse models and human iPSC lines to study the role of epithelial sodium channels (ENaC) and surfactant proteins in lung injury and repair.

Meet Our Team

Research Focus

Lung Injury & Repair

Investigating epithelial sodium channels (ENaC) and surfactant proteins in lung injury, edema, and repair mechanisms using humanized mouse models and iPSC organoids.

COVID-19 & ARDS

Studying the role of fibrinolysis and D-dimers in COVID-19 pathogenesis, with focus on alveolar epithelial injury mechanisms and resolution of pulmonary edema.

Multi-Omics Analysis

Applying cutting-edge scRNAseq, proteomics, and metaproteomics approaches to identify biomarkers and novel therapeutic targets in respiratory diseases.
